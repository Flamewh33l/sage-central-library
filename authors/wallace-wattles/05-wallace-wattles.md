# Wallace Wattles

## Overview
Wallace Delois Wattles (1860-1911) was an American New Thought author best known for "The Science of Getting Rich" (1910). His work focused on the idea that success and wealth could be achieved through specific mental practices, not luck or talent.

## Major Works

### "The Science of Getting Rich" (1910)
- His most famous work
- Influenced by Hinduism and monism
- Claimed wealth follows specific laws like physics

### "The Science of Being Well" (1910)
- Health as a mental science
- Mind-body connection principles

### "The Science of Being Great" (1911)
- Personal development and character building
- Completes the trilogy

## Key Concepts

### The Certain Way
- Success isn't luck — it follows definite laws
- Thinking and acting in "the certain way" produces results
- These laws work for anyone who follows them

### The Substance of Things
- Everything is made from one original substance
- Thought creates form from this substance
- Impressing thought upon this substance attracts what you think about

### Gratitude
- Gratitude connects you to the source
- Ungrateful minds cannot attract good
- The more you're grateful, the more you receive

### Acting in the Present
- Don't wait for future opportunity
- Act NOW with what you have
- Use current circumstances as stepping stones

### The Impression of Increase
- Everyone wants to grow and expand
- Give others the impression of increase
- Add value to every interaction

### Thinking Truth Regardless of Appearances
- Reality isn't what you see; it's what's true underneath
- Think wealth even when appearances show poverty
- This is the hardest part — maintaining vision despite evidence

## Impact on Cognitive Development

1. **Certainty of method** - Success isn't random; it follows patterns
2. **Gratitude as a discipline** - Not just emotion but practice
3. **Present-moment action** - Don't wait for perfect conditions
4. **Vision vs. appearances** - Holding mental pictures despite contradictory evidence
5. **Value creation** - Always adding to others' lives

## The Wallace Wattles Formula
1. Form a clear mental image of what you want
2. Hold this image with faith and purpose
3. Act on it NOW
4. Give more in use value than you receive in cash value
5. Maintain gratitude

## Historical Context
- Wrote during early 20th century American prosperity
- Influenced by Quaker background and later New Thought
- Lived much of his life in poverty before his breakthrough

## Influence
- Major influence on Rhonda Byrne ("The Secret")
- Cited by Bob Proctor and others
- Foundation of modern "law of attraction" teachings

## Criticisms to Consider
- "Science" in title is misleading — not scientific method
- Materialistic focus on wealth
- Ignores systemic barriers to success
- Survivorship bias
- Monistic philosophy as assertion, not proven

## Connection to Other Authors
- Builds on Thomas Troward's mental science
- Precursor to Napoleon Hill
- Direct influence on 20th century prosperity gospel and self-help
