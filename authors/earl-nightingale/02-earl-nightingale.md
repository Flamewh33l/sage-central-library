# Earl Nightingale

## Overview
Earl Nightingale (1921-1989) was an American radio speaker and author, often called the "Dean of Personal Development." He co-founded Nightingale-Conant, one of the largest self-improvement audio publishers. His work focused on attitude, success, and the power of recorded thought.

## Major Works

### "The Strangest Secret" (1956)
- Gold Record winning spoken word album
- Core message: "We become what we think about"
- One of the first major motivational recordings

### "Lead the Field" (1960)
- Audio program on success principles
- Focus on attitude as the key differentiator

### "Our Changing World" (radio program)
- Daily commentary on life and success
- Ran for decades, reaching millions

## Key Concepts

### "We Become What We Think About"
- The defining principle of his work
- Thoughts shape reality through consistent focus
- The mind is like a garden — it grows what you plant

### Attitude is Everything
- Success is 85% attitude, 15% aptitude (his estimation)
- Circumstances don't determine success — response to circumstances does
- Every person is where they are because of what's in their mind

### The Power of Goals
- A person without a goal is like a ship without a rudder
- Written goals create direction and focus
- Daily review of goals keeps the mind aligned

### Continuous Learning
- The most successful people are perpetual students
- Invest in yourself through books, audio, courses
- "One hour per day of study will put you at the top of your field within 3 years"

## Impact on Cognitive Development

1. **Intentional thought direction** - Not letting the mind wander randomly but steering it deliberately
2. **The habit of daily study** - Consistent small investments compound over time
3. **Goal-setting as mental discipline** - Writing and reviewing goals shapes subconscious focus
4. **Attitude as a skill** - Can be trained, not just inherited
5. **Personal responsibility** - Owning your mental state regardless of external circumstances

## Memorable Quotes
- "Whatever we plant in our subconscious mind and nourish with repetition and emotion will one day become a reality."
- "People with goals succeed because they know where they're going."
- "Everything begins with an idea."

## Criticisms to Consider
- Oversimplification of success (ignoring systemic barriers)
- Heavy focus on individual mindset without addressing environment
- Some claims not empirically validated

## Connection to Other Authors
- Influenced by Napoleon Hill (Think and Grow Rich)
- Influenced Bob Proctor directly
- Foundation for much of modern self-help industry
