# Sun Tzu

## Overview
Sun Tzu (c. 544–496 BCE) was a Chinese military general, strategist, and philosopher, traditionally credited as the author of "The Art of War." His work has influenced military thinking, business strategy, and competitive philosophy for over 2,500 years.

## Major Work

### "The Art of War" (孫子兵法, Sūn Zǐ Bīng Fǎ)
- 13 chapters on military strategy
- Written in the Warring States period
- One of the most influential texts ever written
- Applied far beyond warfare: business, sports, politics, negotiation

## The 13 Chapters

1. **Laying Plans** - Assessment before action
2. **Waging War** - Resources and logistics
3. **Attack by Stratagem** - Winning without fighting
4. **Tactical Dispositions** - Positioning for success
5. **Energy** - Momentum and timing
6. **Weak Points and Strong** - Choosing battles
7. **Maneuvering** - Dealing with distance and terrain
8. **Variation in Tactics** - Adaptability
9. **The Army on the March** - Reading terrain and enemies
10. **Terrain** - Ground advantages
11. **The Nine Situations** - Responding to circumstances
12. **The Attack by Fire** - Special weapons/tactics
13. **The Use of Spies** - Intelligence gathering

## Key Concepts

### Win Without Fighting
- Supreme excellence: breaking resistance without fighting
- The best victory is one that doesn't require battle
- Strategy beats force

### Know Yourself and Your Enemy
- "If you know the enemy and know yourself, you need not fear the result of a hundred battles."
- Self-awareness + enemy awareness = confidence
- Information is power

### Deception
- "All warfare is based on deception."
- Appear weak when strong; strong when weak
- Feign disorder to draw out the enemy
- Never show your true position

### Timing and Positioning
- Strike where unprepared; appear where unexpected
- Choose your battles
- Never attack strength with weakness

### Adaptability
- Water shapes its course according to the ground
- No fixed strategies — respond to conditions
- "The general who wins makes many calculations in his temple before the battle."

### Speed and Efficiency
- Long campaigns exhaust resources
- Victory should be quick
- "There is no instance of a nation benefitting from prolonged warfare."

### Avoid Strength, Strike Weakness
- Don't attack fortified positions
- Find the gap in the armor
- Flow around obstacles

## Impact on Cognitive Development

1. **Strategic thinking** - Planning before acting
2. **Information gathering** - Knowledge as foundation
3. **Psychological awareness** - Understanding opponents
4. **Flexibility** - No rigid plans; adapt to conditions
5. **Efficiency** - Minimum effort for maximum result
6. **Indirect approach** - Sometimes the obvious path is the wrong one
7. **Risk assessment** - Know when to fight and when to retreat

## Application Beyond War

- **Business**: Competitive strategy, market positioning
- **Negotiation**: Information leverage, timing
- **Sports**: Game planning, reading opponents
- **Politics**: Campaign strategy, coalition building
- **Personal conflict**: Picking battles, emotional intelligence

## Memorable Quotes

- "The supreme art of war is to subdue the enemy without fighting."
- "In the midst of chaos, there is also opportunity."
- "Let your plans be dark and impenetrable as night, and when you move, fall like a thunderbolt."
- "Victorious warriors win first and then go to war, while defeated warriors go to war and then seek to win."
- "He who knows when he can fight and when he cannot will be victorious."

## Historical Context
- Warring States period — constant conflict
- Sun Tzu served King Helü of Wu
- Historical existence debated; may be composite figure
- Text compiled over centuries

## Criticisms to Consider
- Military focus can be misapplied to non-zero-sum situations
- Deception isn't always ethical or appropriate
- Can promote paranoia and manipulation
- "Enemy" mindset in contexts that need cooperation

## Connection to Other Authors
- Complements Marcus Aurelius (self-knowledge)
- Influenced Miyamoto Musashi
- Applied by modern business strategists
- Foundation for competitive theory
