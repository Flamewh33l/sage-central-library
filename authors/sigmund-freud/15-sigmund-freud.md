# Sigmund Freud

## Overview
Sigmund Freud (1856–1939) was an Austrian neurologist and the founder of psychoanalysis. His theories about the unconscious mind, repression, dreams, and human sexuality fundamentally shaped how we think about psychology, even though many of his specific claims have been disputed or discredited. He remains one of the most influential thinkers of the 20th century.

## Major Works

### "The Interpretation of Dreams" (1899)
- Dreams as "royal road to the unconscious"
- Wish fulfillment theory
- Dream symbolism and analysis

### "Three Essays on the Theory of Sexuality" (1905)
- Human sexuality from childhood
- Libido theory
- Sexual development stages

### "Civilization and Its Discontents" (1930)
- Tension between individual desires and social requirements
- Why civilization creates unhappiness
- Death drive and aggression

### "The Psychopathology of Everyday Life" (1901)
- Slips of the tongue (Freudian slips)
- Forgetfulness as meaningful
- The unconscious in daily life

### "Beyond the Pleasure Principle" (1920)
- Death drive (Thanatos)
- Repetition compulsion
- Pleasure principle vs. reality principle

### "Moses and Monotheism" (1939)
- Religion as collective neurosis
- Historical speculation about Moses
- Final major work

## Key Concepts

### The Unconscious Mind
- Most mental activity occurs below awareness
- Repressed thoughts, desires, memories
- "The unconscious is the true psychical reality."
- Iceberg model: conscious, preconscious, unconscious

### The Structure of the Psyche

**The Id**
- Primitive, instinctual, unconscious
- Pleasure principle
- Demands immediate gratification
- "It is the dark, inaccessible part of our personality."

**The Ego**
- Rational, conscious mediator
- Reality principle
- Balances id, superego, and external world
- "The ego represents what may be called reason and common sense."

**The Superego**
- Internalized moral standards
- Conscience and ego ideal
- Source of guilt and shame
- Critical, judgmental

### Defense Mechanisms
- **Repression** — Burying unacceptable thoughts
- **Denial** — Refusing to accept reality
- **Projection** — Attributing your feelings to others
- **Displacement** — Redirecting emotions to safer targets
- **Sublimation** — Channeling impulses into acceptable activities
- **Rationalization** — Creating logical excuses for irrational behavior
- **Regression** — Returning to earlier developmental stage

### Psychosexual Development

1. **Oral Stage** (0-1) — Mouth as pleasure center
2. **Anal Stage** (1-3) — Toilet training, control
3. **Phallic Stage** (3-6) — Genitals, Oedipus complex
4. **Latency Stage** (6-puberty) — Sexual dormancy
5. **Genital Stage** (puberty-adult) — Mature sexuality

Fixation at any stage creates adult personality issues.

### The Oedipus Complex
- Child desires opposite-sex parent
- Sees same-sex parent as rival
- Must resolve this to develop properly
- Electra complex for girls (term added later by Jung)

### Dreams
- Manifest content (surface story)
- Latent content (hidden meaning)
- Dreams as wish fulfillment
- Dream work transforms latent to manifest

### The Death Drive (Thanatos)
- Instinct toward destruction and return to inorganic state
- Opposes Eros (life instinct)
- Explains aggression, repetition compulsion, self-destructive behavior
- His most controversial concept

## Impact on Cognitive Development

1. **Self-awareness of unconscious** — We don't know our own minds fully
2. **Defense mechanism recognition** — Understanding how we deceive ourselves
3. **Childhood influence** — Early experiences shape adult psychology
4. **Dream interpretation** — Sleep as meaningful, not random
5. **Therapeutic conversation** — Talking as treatment
6. **Symbolic thinking** — Hidden meanings in everyday life
7. **Acceptance of complexity** — Human motivation is layered and contradictory

## Historical Context
- Victorian era sexual repression
- Rise of scientific psychology
- Jewish intellectual in Vienna
- Fled Nazis to London in 1938
- Died by assisted suicide (cancer)

## Criticisms (Substantial)

### Scientific Validity
- Many theories unfalsifiable
- Case studies, not controlled experiments
- Confirmation bias in interpretations
- "Freud was a fraud" school of thought

### Gender and Sexuality
- Penis envy theory widely discredited
- Female psychology poorly understood
- Heteronormative assumptions
- Misunderstanding of sexual trauma

### Cultural Bias
- Victorian Viennese assumptions universalized
- Middle-class neuroses treated as universal
- Ignores social and economic factors

### Therapeutic Effectiveness
- Psychoanalysis often lengthy and ineffective
- Other therapies (CBT) show better outcomes
- Insight doesn't always equal change

## What Remains Valuable
- The unconscious mind (now empirically supported)
- Defense mechanisms (observable in daily life)
- Childhood influence on adult psychology
- Talking therapy as concept
- Human complexity and contradiction

## Influence
- Founded psychotherapy as field
- Influenced art, literature, film
- Transformed how we talk about ourselves
- Terms: Freudian slip, anal retentive, repression, projection
- Even critics work in his shadow

## Connection to Other Authors
- Influenced by Nietzsche (instincts, drives)
- Influenced psychology (Jung split from him)
- Influenced literature (stream of consciousness)
- Marx + Freud = Frankfurt School (Fromm, etc.)
