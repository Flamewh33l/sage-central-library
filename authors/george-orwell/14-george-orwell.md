# George Orwell

## Overview
George Orwell (born Eric Arthur Blair, 1903–1950) was an English novelist, essayist, journalist, and critic. His work is characterized by lucid prose, awareness of social injustice, opposition to totalitarianism, and commitment to democratic socialism. "Animal Farm" and "1984" are among the most influential political novels ever written.

## Major Works

### "Nineteen Eighty-Four" (1949)
- Dystopian masterpiece
- Big Brother, Newspeak, Thought Police, doublethink
- "War is peace. Freedom is slavery. Ignorance is strength."

### "Animal Farm" (1945)
- Allegorical novella
- Farm animals revolt, then become what they overthrew
- "All animals are equal, but some animals are more equal than others."

### "Homage to Catalonia" (1938)
- Memoir of Spanish Civil War
- Betrayal of revolution by communists
- Eyewitness to ideological warfare

### "The Road to Wigan Pier" (1937)
- Investigation of poverty in northern England
- Documentary and polemic
- Social class analysis

### "Down and Out in Paris and London" (1933)
- Memoir of poverty
- Working-class experience
- First book published under "George Orwell"

### "Politics and the English Language" (1946)
- Essay on clear writing
- Political language as tool of deception
- Rules for honest prose

## Key Concepts

### Totalitarianism
- Total control of society by the state
- Not just political but psychological domination
- History rewritten, language controlled, thought policed
- "If you want a picture of the future, imagine a boot stamping on a human face—forever."

### Newspeak
- Language designed to limit thought
- Eliminate words that express dissent
- If you can't say it, you can't think it
- "The purpose of Newspeak was not only to provide a medium of expression for the world-view and mental habits proper to the devotees of Ingsoc, but to make all other modes of thought impossible."

### Doublethink
- Holding two contradictory beliefs simultaneously
- Accepting both as true
- "To know and not to know, to be conscious of complete truthfulness while telling carefully constructed lies."

### Surveillance and Privacy
- Big Brother is always watching
- The panopticon effect — behave as if watched
- Privacy as prerequisite for freedom
- "Big Brother is watching you."

### Memory and History
- "Who controls the past controls the future. Who controls the present controls the past."
- Rewriting history to serve current power
- The mutability of recorded fact

### The Corruption of Revolution
- Animal Farm: revolutionaries become oppressors
- Power corrupts ideals
- "The creatures outside looked from pig to man, and from man to pig, and from pig to man again; but already it was impossible to say which was which."

### Political Language
- Language designed to make lies sound truthful
- Euphemism as tool of power
- "Political language... is designed to make lies sound truthful and murder respectable, and to give an appearance of solidity to pure wind."

## Impact on Cognitive Development

1. **Media literacy** — Recognizing propaganda techniques
2. **Language awareness** — How words shape thought
3. **Historical skepticism** — Questioning official narratives
4. **Totalitarian recognition** — Seeing patterns before they fully manifest
5. **Value of privacy** — Understanding why it matters
6. **Cognitive dissonance** — Recognizing doublethink in ourselves
7. **Clear thinking** — The connection between clear language and clear thought

## Orwell's Rules for Writing

From "Politics and the English Language":
1. Never use a metaphor, simile, or other figure of speech which you are used to seeing in print.
2. Never use a long word where a short one will do.
3. If it is possible to cut a word out, always cut it out.
4. Never use the passive where you can use the active.
5. Never use a foreign phrase, a scientific word, or a jargon word if you can think of an everyday English equivalent.
6. Break any of these rules sooner than say anything outright barbarous.

## Memorable Quotes

- "In a time of deceit, telling the truth is a revolutionary act."
- "The further a society drifts from truth, the more it will hate those who speak it."
- "Freedom is the freedom to say that two plus two make four. If that is granted, all else follows."
- "Political language is designed to make lies sound truthful and murder respectable."
- "The most effective way to destroy people is to deny and obliterate their own understanding of their history."
- "Perhaps a lunatic was simply a minority of one."

## Historical Context
- Fought in Spanish Civil War (nearly killed)
- Saw rise of fascism and Stalinism
- Worked for BBC during WWII (propaganda experience)
- Died of tuberculosis at 46
- Wrote "1984" while terminally ill

## Criticisms to Consider
- Some argue he was anti-left (he wasn't — he was anti-totalitarian)
- Representations of certain groups have been criticized
- Focus on totalitarianism may miss other forms of oppression
- Some view him as overly pessimistic

## Influence
- Terms: Big Brother, Thought Police, Room 101, Orwellian
- Surveillance discourse
- Political language analysis
- Foundation of modern dystopian genre

## Connection to Other Authors
- Solzhenitsyn (totalitarianism from different angles)
- Huxley (Brave New World — different dystopian vision)
- Precursor to modern media criticism
- Applies to digital age surveillance
