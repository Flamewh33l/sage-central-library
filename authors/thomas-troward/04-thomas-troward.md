# Thomas Troward

## Overview
Thomas Troward (1847-1916) was a British judge and philosopher, known for his writings on mental science and the New Thought movement. His work deeply influenced later self-help and metaphysical authors. He applied logical, judicial thinking to spiritual and mental principles.

## Major Works

### "The Edinburgh Lectures on Mental Science" (1908)
- His most influential work
- Systematic approach to understanding mind and reality
- Delivered as lectures in Edinburgh, Scotland

### "The Dore Lectures on Mental Science" (1909)
- Expansion on Edinburgh Lectures
- More practical applications

### "The Creative Process in the Individual" (1915)
- How creative power works through the individual
- Connection between universal mind and personal mind

### "Bible Mystery and Bible Meaning" (1913)
- Metaphysical interpretation of scripture
- Allegorical reading of biblical stories

## Key Concepts

### The Universal Mind
- There is one universal creative mind
- Individual minds are expressions of this universal mind
- Understanding this connection unlocks creative power

### The Law of Growth
- "Whatever you think upon grows"
- Attention is the creative force
- You become what you contemplate

### The Creative Process
- Thought precedes manifestation
- The subconscious is the creative mechanism
- Conscious thought directs, subconscious executes

### Subjective and Objective Mind
- Objective mind: reasoning, analyzing, choosing
- Subjective mind: feeling, believing, creating
- Harmony between the two is essential

### The Principle of Flexibility
- Rigid thinking blocks creative flow
- Flexibility allows new possibilities
- "The flexible survive; the rigid break"

## Impact on Cognitive Development

1. **Understanding mind as creative force** - Thoughts aren't passive; they're formative
2. **The role of attention** - What you focus on expands
3. **Harmony between conscious and subconscious** - Intellect and intuition working together
4. **Flexibility in thinking** - Rigid beliefs limit possibilities
5. **Systematic metaphysics** - Applying logic to spiritual concepts

## Unique Contribution
Troward was unusual in New Thought for his rigorous, logical approach. As a judge, he valued evidence and systematic reasoning. He tried to make metaphysical claims intellectually defensible.

## Influence
- Directly influenced Genevieve Behrend (his student)
- Influenced Ernest Holmes (Science of Mind)
- Cited by Napoleon Hill
- Foundation for much of "law of attraction" teaching

## Criticisms to Consider
- Metaphysical claims not empirically testable
- "Universal mind" is a philosophical assertion, not proven fact
- Potential for magical thinking
- Can lead to victim-blaming (if you created your reality, you're responsible for your suffering)

## Connection to Other Authors
- Precursor to Napoleon Hill, Bob Proctor
- Bridge between philosophy and self-help
- Logical foundation for later metaphysical writers
