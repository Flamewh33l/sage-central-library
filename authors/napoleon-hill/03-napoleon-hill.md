# Napoleon Hill

## Overview
Napoleon Hill (1883-1970) was an American self-help author, best known for "Think and Grow Rich" (1937). He was commissioned by Andrew Carnegie to study successful people and distill their principles into a formula for success. His work became the foundation of modern personal development.

## Major Works

### "Think and Grow Rich" (1937)
- Sold over 70 million copies
- Result of 20+ years of research interviewing successful people
- 13 principles of success

### "The Law of Success" (1928)
- His first major work, expanded into Think and Grow Rich
- More comprehensive, 16 lessons

### "Outwitting the Devil" (1938, published 2011)
- Controversial interview with "the Devil" about fear and drift
- Written during the Depression, deemed too controversial at the time

## The 13 Principles (from Think and Grow Rich)

1. **Desire** - The starting point of all achievement
2. **Faith** - Visualization of and belief in attainment of desire
3. **Autosuggestion** - Influencing the subconscious mind
4. **Specialized Knowledge** - Personal experiences or observations
5. **Imagination** - The workshop of the mind
6. **Organized Planning** - Crystallization of desire into action
7. **Decision** - Overcoming procrastination
8. **Persistence** - Sustained effort necessary to induce faith
9. **Power of the Master Mind** - The driving force
10. **The Mystery of Sex Transmutation** - Channeling energy
11. **The Subconscious Mind** - The connecting link
12. **The Brain** - A broadcasting and receiving station for thought
13. **The Sixth Sense** - The door to the temple of wisdom

## Key Concepts

### Definiteness of Purpose
- Know exactly what you want
- Write it down
- Set a deadline
- This clarity drives all other decisions

### The Master Mind
- No one succeeds alone
- Coordinating knowledge and effort with others
- "Two minds come together, creating a third invisible force"

### Persistence
- Most people give up right before success
- Temporary defeat is not failure
- Every adversity carries the seed of equivalent benefit

### Fear
- The Six Ghosts of Fear: poverty, criticism, ill health, loss of love, old age, death
- Fear is a state of mind that can be controlled
- Indecision breeds doubt, which breeds fear

## Impact on Cognitive Development

1. **Clear goal-setting methodology** - Specificity in defining what you want
2. **Persistence as learned skill** - Not giving up when facing setbacks
3. **Master mind principle** - Recognizing the value of collective intelligence
4. **Fear analysis** - Naming and examining fears reduces their power
5. **Mental transmutation** - Redirecting energy from one purpose to another

## Historical Context
- Interviewed Ford, Edison, Roosevelt, and other titans of industry
- Wrote during the Great Depression
- Later faced criticism for some claims about Carnegie connection

## Criticisms to Consider
- Anecdotal evidence rather than scientific method
- Some historical claims disputed
- Survivorship bias (studying winners without comparing to those who tried and failed)
- "Law of attraction" elements lack empirical support

## Influence
- Foundation for Bob Proctor, Earl Nightingale, and modern self-help
- "Think and Grow Rich" remains one of the best-selling books of all time
