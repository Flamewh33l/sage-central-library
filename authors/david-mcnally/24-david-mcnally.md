# David McNally

## Overview
David McNally is a Canadian author, speaker, and business consultant known for his work on motivation, personal development, and corporate storytelling. His books blend inspiration with practical advice, focusing on finding purpose and making meaningful contributions.

## Major Works

### "Even Eagles Need a Push" (1992)
- His most famous work
- Motivational principles for personal growth
- "Why does the eagle push its young from the nest?"

### "The Eagle's Secret" (1997)
- Sequel to "Even Eagles Need a Push"
- Deeper exploration of success principles
- Building on earlier concepts

### "Your Life is Your Message" (with Karl Ahlrichs)
- Finding meaning in work and life
- Contribution as path to fulfillment

## Key Concepts

### The Push
- Growth requires leaving comfort zones
- The eagle pushes eaglets from the nest — they must fly or fall
- "When the eagle stirs up her nest, she does it because she loves her young."
- We need challenges to grow

### Purpose and Contribution
- Success comes from serving others
- Find what you can contribute
- "Your life is your message to the world. Make it inspiring."
- Self-actualization through service

### The Four Principles (from Even Eagles Need a Push)

1. **Commitment** — Total dedication to your purpose
2. **Control** — Taking charge of your attitudes and actions
3. **Challenge** — Embracing difficulties as opportunities
4. **Contribution** — Making a difference for others

### Attitude as Choice
- We can't control circumstances, only responses
- "Life is 10% what happens to you and 90% how you respond to it."
- Optimism as discipline, not personality

### The Comfort Zone
- Safety prevents growth
- "The only way to discover the limits of the possible is to go beyond them into the impossible."
- Stagnation vs. growth

### Meaningful Work
- Work should align with values
- Contribution through career
- "Your work is your gift to the world."

## Impact on Cognitive Development

1. **Growth mindset** — Challenges as opportunities
2. **Service orientation** — Success through contribution
3. **Personal responsibility** — Owning your responses
4. **Purpose alignment** — Connecting daily work to deeper meaning
5. **Comfort zone awareness** — Recognizing when safety becomes stagnation
6. **Attitude discipline** — Choosing perspective deliberately
7. **Legacy thinking** — What message does your life send?

## Memorable Quotes

- "Your life is your message to the world. Make it inspiring."
- "When the eagle stirs up her nest, she does it because she loves her young."
- "The only way to discover the limits of the possible is to go beyond them into the impossible."
- "Commitment is the stuff character is made of; the power to change the face of things. It is the daily triumph of integrity over skepticism."

## Connection to Self-Help Tradition
- Follows Napoleon Hill tradition
- Corporate motivational speaking
- Blend of inspiration and practical advice
- Focus on contribution distinguishes from pure self-interest

## Criticisms to Consider
- Corporate motivational genre has limits
- Some find eagle metaphor overused
- Individual focus may ignore structural barriers
- Success requires more than attitude

## Value
The emphasis on contribution over extraction — success as service rather than selfishness — distinguishes his work from more purely individualistic self-help.
